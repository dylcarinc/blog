**Breaking down the problem**

Once reading the description, I felt that I could break down the problem
into a few different entities to handle different parts of the grocery
store. First, we can have an entity for the items that in the store,
with their name price, description, quantity available, and
manufacturer, along with a product id. The customers would be a separate
entity, with them having a name, address and phone number, and a
customer id. The orders could be a third entity with the id of the
customer, and an id for the order, along with its time and date, and if
the order is finalized. An order can be represented by another entity
containing each item, its quantity, special instructions, and
substitutions which would be represented by a product id.

**User stories**

- As the manager of the store's inventory, I need to be able to change
  the quantity of items.

  - This could be connected to the orders made by customers, as it would
    decrease the quantity of the item. This would be a major part of the
    project.

- As a customer, I would like to register for an account to make orders.

  - We would need to hold their name, address, and phone number. This
    should take less time in the project and will be a starting point
    for building our database.

- As an employee, I need to know the order of the customer.

  - This will involve building out the order entity in the database,
    which will be a major part of the project.

- As an employee, I need to know what orders I need to fulfill for the
  day.

  - This will involve building out an entity that represents all of the
    orders.

- As a customer, I need to change my order before finalizing it

  - This would involve the orders entity and be a large part of the
    project.

**Reflection**

I am still a bit unsure with my flowchart and schema. I think that my
attributes are correct, but I may not have completely correct
relationships between them.
